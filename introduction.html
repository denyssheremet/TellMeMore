What is this game about?

Abstractions

When people communicate, they often use abstract words like "technology", "growing" and "disruption".

Those words may sound like they mean something specific, but there are a lot of different ways to interpret them.

Does "technology" refer to phones? Fiber-optic cables? Quantum computers? Or something else entirely?

You have no way of knowing... until you ask them.

Illusion of Understanding

One of the major roadblocks to communication is the illusion of understanding. That happens when two people are talking about technology, and one is thinking about fiber-optic cables, while the other is thinking of quantum computers.

Or when two people are talking about that there needs to be a disruption in education, but one is thinking about giving students more freedom, while the other is thinking about how to train his dog to sit using carrots.

Both people are using the same words, but they derive a completely different meaning from those words. And they won't find out until they ask specifying questions.

And you can learn to do that using this game.

(Most of the time the misunderstandings are less obvious than in the examples above.)
